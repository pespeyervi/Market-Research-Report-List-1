<p><p>The tuberculosis vaccination market is primarily categorized into immune vaccines and therapy vaccines. Immune vaccines are designed to prevent infection by eliciting a strong immune response against Mycobacterium tuberculosis. In contrast, therapy vaccines aim to enhance immune responses in individuals already infected, helping to control or eliminate the disease. Both types play critical roles in managing tuberculosis, with immune vaccines focusing on prevention and therapy vaccines targeting treatment and recovery in infected patients.</p></p>
